# 5-Day Training Split — Advanced Lifter, Cut / Recomp

**Goal:** Stay lean while in a calorie deficit. Preserve muscle and strength.
**Philosophy:** Fat loss is driven by the *diet*, not the training. So we keep
**primary lifts HEAVY (5–8 reps)** to protect muscle and the nervous system, and
use **hypertrophy rep ranges (8–15) on accessories** for volume and metabolic
work. No "toning"/endless high-rep circuits.

**Split logic (Push / Pull / Legs A / Upper / Legs B):** no muscle group is
trained hard on two consecutive days, lateral delts get direct work twice
(seated + standing), and weekly volume lands in the 10–20 hard-set band per
muscle.

> 🔒 **Muscle-preservation reminder:** In a deficit, *intensity is your insurance* — keep loads heavy on the big lifts, hit your protein (~2.0–2.4 g/kg), and don't chase the burn; chase the same/heavier numbers.

## Conditioning (low-impact, deficit-friendly)

- **2–3× / week, 15–25 min** of *low-impact* steady-state on off-feet modalities
  (incline walk, bike, or sled pushes if available). Keep it Zone 2 — conversational pace.
- Optional **8–10 min** of easy cardio post-lift to aid recovery/appetite control.
- **Avoid** high-intensity intervals on heavy-lift days in a deficit — they steal
  recovery you need to keep the big lifts heavy. Put any harder cardio on a rest day.
- Walk **8–10k steps/day**; this is your real fat-loss lever, not gym cardio.
